# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary demo viewer is a hackathon judge or investor evaluating whether Carry can become a credible product. The product framing should still speak to doctors, clinic owners, and healthcare operators who need less administrative burden and safer continuity across visits.

Users are viewing a customer-facing web demo, not a developer console. They need to quickly understand the workflow, trust the system, and see how ambient clinical conversations become reviewed notes, patient context, follow-ups, and tool actions.

## Product Purpose

Carry is a voice-first assistant that carries context from professional conversations into notes, memory, follow-ups, and work actions.

For this demo, Carry shows Doctor Mode deeply: it listens to a simulated clinical conversation, privacy-filters sensitive transcript content, tracks clinical changes live, drafts clinician-review artifacts, stores safe patient memory, and pushes documentation into external tools such as Notion.

Success means the viewer believes three things:

1. Carry saves doctors time on documentation.
2. Carry safely captures clinical context across visits.
3. Carry detects important clinical changes live, then turns them into reviewable work actions.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, premium.

Carry should feel like a trusted clinical command center: quiet, organized, privacy-aware, and competent. The interface should create confidence without looking sterile, generic, or overproduced.

## Anti-references

Carry should not look like:

- Neon AI dashboards.
- Busy hospital admin portals.
- Chatbot-style interfaces.
- Debug console layouts.
- Generic SaaS gradient-card templates.
- Emoji-heavy hackathon demos.
- Overwhelming analytics dashboards that bombard the viewer with every possible data point.

## Design Principles

1. Progressive disclosure over data dumps: show the right layer at the right moment.
2. Review-first clinical trust: every clinical artifact is a draft that clearly requires clinician review.
3. Continuity is the demo: emphasize what Carry remembers and carries forward across visits.
4. Actions are accountable: show what was proposed, what was completed, and what still needs review.
5. Privacy is visible but quiet: make de-identification obvious without making the interface feel alarmist.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, focus states, and semantic structure. Avoid color-only meaning for safety, review, and completion states. Support reduced motion by keeping transitions short and functional. Use clear clinical language and avoid decorative symbols or emoji.
